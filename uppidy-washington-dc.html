Uppidy

What would you do if you unexpectedly lost all of the text messages saved on your phone? Could you get them back from your phone service provider such as AT&T or Verizon?

The answer, shockingly, is no. Your own personal data is actually the property of your phone service provider. There is no way to retrieve that data unless you have a court subpoena. Even with a subpoena the service provider will only grant you one week's history of your own personal records.

What if one of the text message logs you lost was with a friend who died suddenly a short time after your conversation? There must be a better solution to this problem, especially as our communications increasingly become purely digital.
